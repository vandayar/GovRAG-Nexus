Executive Retrospective: Project GovRAG-Nexus
Period: Q1 2026

Lead: Phartheeb Tanjore Kandasamy

Domain: AI Governance & QE Transformation

1. The Challenge: "Soft Language" Risk
Our legacy Wealth Management policies contained "compliance gaps" where data retention periods (e.g., 7 years) were defined without specific Trigger Dates. Standard RAG systems flagged these as "Passed," creating a latent regulatory risk. Additionally, the deprecation of legacy AI models in March 2026 threatened the stability of our audit infrastructure.

2. The Strategic Solution
I led a QE-driven migration to a Multi-Agent architecture using Gemini 2.5 Flash.

Infrastructure Resilience: Resolved the gemini-1.5-flash sunset by re-architecting LCEL chains for the 2026 production standard.

Adaptive Auditing: Developed a "Thinking" Auditor Agent that assigns a 1-10 Risk Score based on linguistic precision, not just keyword matching.

3. Quantifiable Impact (ROI)
Risk Reduction: Successfully identified and "hardened" vague clauses, moving the institutional risk profile from 8/10 (High) to 3/10 (Low).

Operational Velocity: Automated the "Clarity Audit" process, reducing manual legal review time for new policy drafts by an estimated 65%.

Continuous Governance: Implemented an Anomaly Alert system that triggers a "High Priority" remediation workflow if policy drift is detected in future updates.

4. Lessons for AI Growth
This project proves that AI Governance must be dynamic. By moving to a multi-agent model, we've built a system that doesn't just "read" policies—it defends them against ambiguity.
